Structured Text

The structured content format I studied was DITA, an XML-based tool for authoring and publishing technical documents and Oxygen, a popular DITA editor. I used them to create a salsa recipe.

DITA example
<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<!DOCTYPE task PUBLIC "-//OASIS//DTD DITA General Task//EN" "generalTask.dtd">
<task id="task_wbx_wys_ybb">
    <title>A Simple Salsa is the Best Salsa</title>
    <shortdesc>Simple recipe for salsa cruda</shortdesc>
    <taskbody>
        <context>
            <p>3-4 medium tomatoes</p>
            <p>3-4 serrano peppers (5-6, if you are me)</p>
            <p>1 bunch of green onions</p>
            <p>several cloves of garlic</p>
            <p>1 bunch of cilantro</p>
            <p>salt</p>
            <p>pepper</p>
            <p>honey</p>
        </context>
        <steps>
            <step>
                <cmd>
                    Dice the tomatoes into a glass or plastic bowl.
                </cmd>
                <info>
                    Optionally, you can chop one tor two of the tomatoes in a food processor or blender,
                    but keep most of them in chunk form.
                </info>
            </step>
            <step>
                <cmd>
                    Dice the peppers and green onions and finely chop the garlic.
                </cmd>
            </step>
            <step>
                <cmd>Remove the stems from the cilantro and chop the leaves.
                </cmd>
            </step>
            <step>
                <cmd>Finish with a pinch of salt, a pinch of pepper (to taste) and a light drizzle of the honey â€“ not
                    more than half a tablespoon.</cmd>
            </step>
            <step>
                <cmd>Stir everything together well and refrigerate for a minimum of thirty minutes.</cmd>
            </step>
            <step>
                <cmd>Serve with fresh tortilla chips.</cmd>
            </step>
        </steps>
    </taskbody>
</task>
